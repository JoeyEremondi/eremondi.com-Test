About

Hi, I am Rachit Nigam. I am a second year student at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst, slated to graduate in 2019. I am interested in program synthesis, type theory and formal verification of programming languages.

I am drawn to programming language research because of the strong foundational mathematics it is built on and the many real world applications that the tools made possible due to PL research have. I work with Prof. Arjun Guha as a research intern in the PLASMA lab.

Research

I am currently working on extending Rehearsal by building a program capable of synthesizing shell scripts from I/O traces and file-system logs with Arjun Guha.

While I am still new to research, I hope to build tools that are capable of solving real world problems, while providing strong formal guarentees. I am particular interested in the field of program synthesis and learning systems. I also enjoy implementing smaller prototypes of tools presented in PL literature. For example, I recently implemented the algorithm given by Dana Angluin to learn regular expressions from queries and counterexamples.
